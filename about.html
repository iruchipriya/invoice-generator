Our Story

It started with a missed payment.

A freelance graphic designer had just wrapped up a three-week branding project. The client was happy. The work was done. But when it came time to send an invoice, they spent 45 frustrating minutes wrestling with Microsoft Word — tables shifting, alignment breaking, totals that had to be calculated manually. By the time the PDF was finally ready, it looked unprofessional and the client took weeks to pay.

"I spent more time formatting the invoice than I did on the actual design. There had to be a better way."

That experience is not unique. Millions of freelancers, independent contractors, and small business owners around the world face the same problem every month. Either they use expensive accounting software that requires a subscription, or they cobble together a document in Word or Google Docs and hope it looks professional enough to get paid on time.

InvoiceGen was built to fix this. We created a free, browser-based invoice generator that produces a clean, professional PDF in under two minutes — with no account, no software to install, no subscription fee, and no watermark on the finished document. The entire tool runs inside your browser, which means your client data and invoice details never leave your device.

Since launching, InvoiceGen has been used to create invoices in over 150 countries, supporting currencies from the Indian Rupee and US Dollar to the UAE Dirham, British Pound, Euro, and dozens more. It supports GST, VAT, CGST, SGST, and any custom tax structure — because billing requirements differ by country, and every user deserves a tool that works for their situation.

Our Mission

Our mission is straightforward: professional invoicing should be free and accessible to everyone.

A 19-year-old freelance developer in Bengaluru should be able to send a GST-compliant invoice just as easily as an established agency in New York. A self-employed carpenter in Manchester should not have to pay a monthly subscription just to bill their clients. A consultant in Dubai should be able to switch to AED, add VAT at 5%, and download a PDF without reading a help article.

That is the standard we hold ourselves to with every update we make to InvoiceGen.

The tool is kept free through advertising. Ads appear on the website — but never on your downloaded invoice. Your PDF is clean, professional, and entirely yours.

What We Stand For

Privacy by Default

Your invoice data is processed entirely in your browser. We never see it, never store it, and never send it anywhere. This is not just a policy — it is how the tool is built.

Genuinely Free

No trial. No freemium tier. No watermark unless you pay. InvoiceGen is free for everyone, every time. We sustain the service through advertising, not subscriptions.

Simplicity First

Every feature we add is weighed against one question: does this make it easier to create an invoice? If not, it does not ship. We are a tool, not a platform.

Built for the World

InvoiceGen works for users in India, the UK, the US, the UAE, Australia, Canada, South Africa, and everywhere in between. Currency support and tax flexibility are not afterthoughts.

How We Compare to Alternatives

Most free invoice tools have a catch. Wave requires an account. Invoice Ninja is powerful but complex for simple use cases. Zoho Invoice is part of a much larger product suite that can feel overwhelming. Microsoft Word and Google Docs require manual formatting every single time. Many "free" generators add a visible watermark to your PDF unless you upgrade to a paid plan.

InvoiceGen has no catch. No account. No watermark. No upgrade required. The PDF you download looks exactly like what a designer would produce in hours — but it takes you two minutes. And because everything happens in your browser, you do not need to trust us with your business data. There is nothing to trust — we simply never receive it.

We think that is the way it should be.
